              # Overview

              TIP

              If you are new to trading, you can read our tutorial "Trading basics".

              # Introduction

              A trading bot is a computer program that can automatically place orders to a market or exchange without the need for human intervention. They are working for you 24/7 and never lose their focus.

              Cassandre trading bot (available as a Spring boot starter (opens new window)) allows you to create and execute your trading strategy in seconds on most crypto exchanges. We also provide a dry mode and a spring boot starter to backtest your bot on historical and/or real-time data.

              Once the starter is added to your Spring Boot project, it will search for a class having the @CassandreStrategy (opens new window) annotation and extending BasicCassandreStrategy (opens new window) or BasicTa4jCassandreStrategy (opens new window).

              # BasicCassandreStrategy

              For a BasicCassandreStrategy (opens new window), you have to implement :

              • getRequestedCurrencyPairs() (opens new window) to indicate the list of currency pairs tickers you want to receive.
              • getTradeAccount() (opens new window) to indicate which account is your trading account.

              # BasicTa4jCassandreStrategy

              For a BasicTa4jCassandreStrategy (opens new window), you have to implement :

              • getRequestedCurrencyPair() (opens new window) to indicate the currency pair tickers you want to receive.
              • getTradeAccount() (opens new window) to indicate which account is your trading account.
              • getMaximumBarCount() (opens new window) to indicate how many bars you want to keep.
              • getDelayBetweenTwoBars() (opens new window) to indicate the delay between two bars.
              • getStrategy() (opens new window) to implement your own strategy.
              • shouldEnter() (opens new window) to indicate what you want to do when it's time to buy.
              • shouldExit() (opens new window) to indicate what you want to do when it's time to sell.

              # Data updates

              To be notified of new data, you can override the following methods :

              • onAccountsUpdates() (opens new window) to receive updates about your account.
              • onTickersUpdates() (opens new window) to receive new tickers.
              • onOrdersUpdates() (opens new window) to receive updates about your orders.
              • onTradesUpdates() (opens new window) to receive updates about your trades.
              • onPositionsUpdates() (opens new window) to receive updates about your positions.
              • onPositionsStatusUpdates() (opens new window) to receive updates about position status change.

              # Buying/selling

              Inside your strategy, you can create market orders with the methods :

              • createBuyMarketOrder() (opens new window)
              • createSellMarketOrder() (opens new window)

              And limit orders with :

              • createBuyLimitOrder() (opens new window)
              • createSellLimitOrder() (opens new window)

              # Positions

              You can also create positions with :

              • createLongPosition() (opens new window)
              • createShortPosition() (opens new window)

              On Positions, you can get the:

              • The lowest calculated gain with getLowestPrice() (opens new window)
              • The highest calculated gain with getHighestGainPrice() (opens new window)
              • The latest calculated gain with getLatestGainPrice() (opens new window)

              On a closed position, you can get the gain & fees with getGain() (opens new window)

              TIP

              If you don't know what a position is, read this position system explanation.
